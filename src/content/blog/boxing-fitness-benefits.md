I started boxing in 2018 as a complete beginner. By 2019, I had earned a Silver Medal at the South Australian State Championships. Boxing didn't just change my body — it changed who I am.

Here's why I believe boxing fitness is one of the best things you can do for your health.

## 1. Full-Body Workout in Every Session

Boxing engages virtually every muscle in your body:
- **Upper body:** Shoulders, chest, back, arms
- **Core:** Constant rotation and stabilisation
- **Lower body:** Footwork, stance, power generation

You'll burn 400-700 calories in a 60-minute session while building real functional strength.

## 2. Incredible Cardiovascular Conditioning

The interval nature of boxing — bursts of intense activity followed by brief recovery — is one of the most effective forms of cardio for:
- Improving VO2 max
- Burning fat
- Building cardiovascular endurance

## 3. Stress Relief Like Nothing Else

There's something uniquely cathartic about hitting pads. The focus required to execute combinations properly forces you to be completely present — no room for work stress or anxiety.

Many of my clients tell me boxing is their therapy.

## 4. Improved Coordination and Reflexes

Boxing requires your brain and body to work together in ways most exercise doesn't. Over time, you'll notice improvements in:
- Hand-eye coordination
- Reaction time
- Body awareness and proprioception

## 5. Mental Toughness and Discipline

Boxing teaches you to push through discomfort, stay focused under pressure, and keep going when you want to quit. These mental skills transfer directly to every other area of your life.

## 6. Confidence and Self-Defence

There's a quiet confidence that comes from knowing you can handle yourself. You don't need to be a fighter — just knowing the basics changes how you carry yourself.

## 7. It's Actually Fun

Let's be honest — most cardio is boring. Boxing is engaging, challenging, and constantly evolving. You'll look forward to your sessions rather than dreading them.
